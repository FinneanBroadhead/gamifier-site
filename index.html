The Uber Game

The Uber Game is an interactive news game that puts you into the shoes of a full-time Uber driver. Based on real reporting, it aims to convey an emotional understanding of what it is like to try to make a living in the gig economy. This game will have much more attention due to its unique approach to a mobile game. Going against the curve, Uber brings us a game more centered on reality, ie: what its like to be in the shoes of a gig economy labor worker. By bringing meaning to a mobile game you will get these publications/major news networks attracting users to the game. This coy strategy pushed uber forward by having their brand advertised not just for 20-30 seconds like TV, but instead advertising upon the user for whatever amount of play time the user plays for.

Gamifications Effect on Your Brains Reward System„


Your brain rewards itself in many different ways, gamification has 2 different lanes of rewards for your brains central nervous system; Enjoyment from the game is one (accomplishment/entertainment) and the other, is material reward. Lets say for example you're at the movie theater with your friends, and one of the charecters make a really funny joke. You and all of your friends start laughing because your brain subconciously told itself to stimulate your dopamine and nor-epinepharne and seritonin receptors, leaving you are your friends are joyful due to the brain rewarding itself. Dopamine and Serotonin are considered the "happy chemicals" of the reward system and when you play a mobile game your brain is proven to release large amounts of these chemicals. Theres a reason games are titled "addicting." The game is similar to a drug, the feeling of being "high" is due to illicit substances causing irregular amounts of these rewards, the difference being with a game all the feelings of satisfaction are caused naturally. How this affects your business„

It's extremely difficult to create an advertisment that is appealing to the consumer. This undeniable fact makes our job here very tedious. However, there is a solution. Gamification grabs the users attention with an interactive advertisment that has completely changed from being a nusciance and a bother, to something engaging and unique. There are many stastics that prove our statement as well, you can find many on the second page. One example is Samsung Nation, launched in 2011 they created a reward and badge system. The increase from this reward system was monumentous, resulting in 500% increase in product reviews, 66% increase in visitors and 300% more comments. The reason this intense change happened is simply due to your brain chemistry. When presented with a reward, your brain cant help but release large amounts of dopamine, and when that happens brain craves that same sensation even more (ie: 500% more reviews and 300% more comments.) A simple ranking system built a massive userbase for samsung, and everyone else seemed to follow suit.
